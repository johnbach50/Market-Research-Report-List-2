<p><p>Submarine optical fiber cables are categorized into two main types based on their installation depth: deep-sea fiber optic cables and shallow sea optic cables. Deep-sea fiber optic cables are designed to be installed in deep ocean waters, typically for connecting continents or countries over long distances. In contrast, shallow sea optic cables are installed in shallower waters closer to the shore, often used to connect offshore platforms, islands, or coastal regions. Each type of cable serves different purposes and requires specific engineering considerations for their installation and operation.</p></p>
