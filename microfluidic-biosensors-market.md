<p><p>Microfluidic biosensors are versatile tools that can be classified into two main types: electrochemical sensors and optical sensors. </p><p>Electrochemical sensors utilize the conversion of target molecules into electrical signals, and are suitable for detecting a wide range of biochemical species. </p><p>On the other hand, optical sensors rely on the interaction of light with target molecules to produce a measurable signal. These sensors are highly sensitive and offer real-time analysis capabilities. Both types of sensors play a crucial role in various applications, such as medical diagnostics and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
