<p><p>Sorghum beer, also known as traditional African beer, is classified into two main market types based on its production process: wet-based and dry-based. Wet-based sorghum beer is made by fermenting a mixture of sorghum flour and water, resulting in a thick, porridge-like consistency. Dry-based sorghum beer, on the other hand, is made by fermenting malted sorghum grains. The main difference between the two types lies in the fermentation process, with wet-based sorghum beer being more common in rural areas and dry-based sorghum beer being more popular in urban markets.</p></p>
