There are two main types of neonatal incubators: transport neonatal incubators, which are portable and designed for use during the transfer of newborns between hospitals or medical facilities, and normal neonatal incubators, which are stationary units used for long-term care and monitoring of infants in neonatal intensive care units (NICUs). Both types play a crucial role in helping to improve the outcomes for vulnerable newborns.</p></p>
