<p><ul><li>Invasive</li><li>Non-invasive</li><li>Partially invasive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Brain Computer Interface (BCI) market is divided into three types based on the level of invasiveness: Invasive, Non-invasive, and Partially invasive. Invasive BCIs involve implanting electrodes directly into the brain, providing high-precision but risky and costly solutions. Non-invasive BCIs use external devices to measure brain activity, offering safer solutions but with lower accuracy. Partially invasive BCIs strike a balance between the two, combining some level of implantation for improved accuracy while minimizing risks associated with invasive procedures. Each type caters to different user needs and applications within the BCI market.</p></p>
