<p><p>Microcrystalline cellulose is available in two main market types: wood-based and non-wood based. Non-wood-based microcrystalline cellulose is produced from alternative sources like cotton linters or plants. Both types of microcrystalline cellulose are widely used as a food additive, pharmaceutical excipient, and in various industrial applications due to their unique properties and versatility.</p></p>
