<p><p>Light Sheet Microscope is a powerful imaging technique used for biological research. One of the types is Selective Plane Illumination Microscopy (SPIM), which uses a thin sheet of light to image biological samples. Another type is Dual-view Plane Illumination Microscopy (DiSPIM), which provides higher resolution and 3D imaging. Other types of light sheet microscopes include Bessel beam plane illumination microscopy and Temporal Focusing Microscopy. These advanced techniques enable researchers to study complex biological processes in living organisms with high resolution and minimal damage.</p></p>
