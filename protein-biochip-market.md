<p><p>Protein biochips are used in the field of proteomics for various applications. There are two main types of protein biochip markets: active chip and passive chip. Active chips have integrated sensors or electrodes that can detect and quantify proteins or other molecules present in a sample. Passive chips, on the other hand, rely on external detection methods such as fluorescence or mass spectrometry to analyze the interactions on the biochip surface. Both types of biochips have unique advantages and are utilized for different research purposes.</p></p>
