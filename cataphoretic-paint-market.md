<p><ul><li>Anodic Electrophoretic Paint</li><li>Cathodic Electrophoretic Paint</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cataphoretic paint, also known as electrophoretic paint, comes in two main types: Anodic Electrophoretic Paint and Cathodic Electrophoretic Paint. Anodic electrophoretic paint is positively charged and adheres to negatively charged metal surfaces, providing excellent corrosion protection. On the other hand, cathodic electrophoretic paint is negatively charged and works best on positively charged metal surfaces. Both types of cataphoretic paint provide durable and long-lasting finishes, making them popular choices in various industries such as automotive, electronics, and appliances.</p></p>
