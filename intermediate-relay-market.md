<p><p>Intermediate relays are devices used to control the flow of electricity in a circuit. There are two main types of intermediate relays: static type and electromagnetic type. </p><p>Static type relays use solid-state components to control the circuit, providing fast and accurate switching of signals. </p><p>Electromagnetic type relays use coils and electromagnets to control the flow of electricity, allowing for higher power handling capabilities. Both types have their own set of advantages and are used in various applications based on their specific characteristics and requirements.</p></p>
