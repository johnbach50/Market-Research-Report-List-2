<p><p>Flame retardant chemicals are classified into two main types - organic and inorganic. Organic flame retardant chemicals are derived from carbon-containing compounds and are commonly used in textiles, plastics, and electronics. Inorganic flame retardant chemicals, on the other hand, are mineral-based and include compounds such as aluminum trihydrate and antimony trioxide. Both types of flame retardant chemicals work by reducing the flammability of materials and slowing down the spread of fires.</p></p>
