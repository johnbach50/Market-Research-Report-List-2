<p><p>Kosher food markets are categorized into three main types: Meat, Dairy, and Pareve. Meat markets exclusively sell kosher-certified animal products, such as beef, chicken, and lamb, that meet Jewish dietary laws. Dairy markets offer a variety of kosher-certified dairy products, including milk, cheese, and yogurt, that are prepared in accordance with traditional guidelines. Pareve markets provide kosher-certified products that are neither meat nor dairy, such as fruits, vegetables, grains, and some processed foods, making them suitable for consumption with both meat and dairy products.</p></p>
