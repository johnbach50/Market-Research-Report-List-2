<p><ul><li>PIR (Passive Infrared) Sensors</li><li>Ultrasonic Sensors</li><li>Dual-technology Occupancy Sensors</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Non-residential occupancy sensors help save energy by controlling lighting and HVAC systems based on occupancy. PIR sensors detect heat and movement, ideal for smaller spaces. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to detect motion, best for areas with obstacles. Dual-technology sensors combine PIR and ultrasonic for higher accuracy. Other types include microwave sensors, photo sensors, and hybrid sensors. Each type offers unique benefits and can be used in various commercial and industrial settings to optimize energy usage and improve occupant comfort.</p></p>
