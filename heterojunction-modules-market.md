<p><p>Heterojunction modules have two main types: N-type and P-type. N-type modules use a thin layer of a negatively doped semiconductor, while P-type modules use a positively doped semiconductor layer. N-type modules have higher efficiency and better performance in low light conditions, while P-type modules are more cost-effective and have a longer lifespan. Both types of modules have unique characteristics and are used in different applications based on their specific advantages and disadvantages in terms of performance, cost, and durability.</p></p>
