<p><p>Colorless short term absorbable suture threads are typically made from materials such as polyglycolic acid or polyglactin. These threads are transparent and are ideal for surgeries where visibility of the wound is important. On the other hand, violet short term absorbable suture threads are dyed with a purple color for easy identification and removal. Surgeons typically use violet threads when they need to distinguish between different types of sutures or when working on multiple layers of tissue. Both types of threads are absorbed by the body over time, eliminating the need for removal.</p></p>
